Learnpedia Products for Business Technical Support Services Guidelines

The following technical support services guidelines ("Guidelines") apply to support services for your Learnpedia Products for Business Agreement entered into by and between Learnpedia and Customer (as may be applicable, the "Agreement").

1. Support Request Submission

  • 1.1 Customer Efforts to Fix Errors. Prior to making a request to Learnpedia, Customer will use reasonable efforts to fix any error, bug, malfunction or network connectivity defect without escalation to Learnpedia. Thereafter, a Customer Contact may submit a written request for technical support through the online help center or support portal as provided by Learnpedia, or by phone.
  • 1.2 Characterization of Requests. Customers designate priority upon submission of Requests. Upon receiving a request from Customer Contact, Learnpedia will determine whether the request is a "Service Unusable," "Standard Request" or a "Feature Request" (as defined in Section 5). Any such determination made by Learnpedia is final and binding on Customer. Learnpedia reserves the right to change Customer's Priority designation if Learnpedia believes that Customer's designation is incorrect and will inform Customer of any such change in its response to the support Request. Customer may appeal any such reclassification to Learnpedia's Support management for review through any available support channel.
  • 1.3 Procedures for Acknowledgement and Resolution of Requests. When making a Request, Customer will provide requested diagnostic information including but not limited to: (i) describing the problem, the configuration, and Customer's network; (ii) providing relevant data; and (iii) communicating further via email or telephone to answer questions and assist Learnpedia Support Personnel as appropriate.
  • 1.4 Request Acknowledgement. Learnpedia may respond to a Request by acknowledging receipt of the Request. Customer acknowledges and understands that Learnpedia may be unable to provide answers to, or resolve all, Requests.
  • 1.5 Feature Requests. If Learnpedia deems a Request as a Feature Request, Learnpedia will log such Request for consideration to add to a future update or release of the Services and will consider the matter closed. Learnpedia is under no obligation to respond to or resolve any Feature Request or to include any such Feature Request in any future update or release.
  • 2. Accessing Support
  • 2.1 Learnpedia Help Center. Customer's End Users of the Learnpedia Products for Business Services are provided support solely through the Learnpedia.com Help Center, which is accessible at ${cmdsHelpUrl} and ${learnHelpUrl} or such URL as Learnpedia may provide. Customer is responsible for responding to any questions and complaints by End Users or other third parties relating to Customer's or its End Users' use of the Services, with such support services to be provided at Customer's own expense.
  • 2.2 Support Hours and Target Initial Response Times. Learnpedia will provide access to help center for customers on a 24 x 7 basis. P1 Priority support Requests are responded to with a target initial response time of one hour and are responded to 24 x 7. If contact is made for a P1 Priority support Request on a weekend or applicable holiday or non-business hours of the location to which the Requests are assigned, a phone call is needed to trigger a return support response. P2, P3 & P4 Priority support Requests are responded to during business hours of the location to which the Requests are assigned. P2 Priority support Requests will be responded to with an initial target response time of 1 business day or less.
  • 3. Standard Support All Customers will receive Standard Support which includes the following:
  • * Automatic product upgrades of the Services
  • * Maintenance updates of the Services
  • * Online self help and training for End Users and Administrators designed to assist Customers with implementation and use of the Services
  • * Access to support by phone and web
  • 4. General Provisions
  • 4.1 Updates to Guidelines. Learnpedia may update these Guidelines from time to time but any update will not have a material adverse effect on Customer.
  • 4.2 Maintenance. To ensure optimal performance of the Services, Learnpedia performs periodic Maintenance. In most cases, Maintenance will have limited or no negative impact on the availability and functionality of the Services. If Learnpedia expects planned Maintenance to negatively affect the availability or functionality of the Services, Learnpedia will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide at least two days advance notice of the Maintenance. In addition, Learnpedia may perform emergency unscheduled Maintenance at any time. If Learnpedia expects such emergency unscheduled Maintenance to negatively affect the availability or functionality of the Services, Learnpedia will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide advance notice of such Maintenance. Maintenance notices noted above will be provided via the Admin Tool and/or Support Portal or email to the Notification Email Address provided by the Customer at the time of signing up.
  • 4.3 Language. The parties agree that all support provided by Learnpedia to Customer pursuant to these Guidelines will be provided in the English language.
  • 4.4 Term of Support. Learnpedia will only provide the support services described in these Guidelines during the term of the Agreement and will have no obligation to provide any support services to Customer after the expiration or termination of such Agreement.
  • 5. Glossary of Definitions
  • For the purpose of these Guidelines, the capitalized terms below will have the following meanings:
  • 5.1 "Customer Contacts" means Administrators designated in the Admin Tool.
  • 5.2 "Feature Request" means a Request by a Customer Contact to incorporate a new feature or enhance an existing feature of the Services that is currently not available as part of the existing Services.
  • 5.3 "Learnpedia Support Personnel" mean the Learnpedia representatives responsible for handling technical support requests.
  • 5.4 "Maintenance" means maintenance work that is performed on hardware or software delivering the Services.
  • 5.5 "Priority" means the level of impact a Request is having on Customer's operations and is used to establish target response times.
  • P1: Critical Impact - Service Unusable in Production
  • P2: High Impact - Service Use Severely Impaired
  • P3: Medium Impact - Service Use Partially Impaired
  • P4: Low Impact - Service Fully Usable
  • 5.6 "Request" means a request from Customer to Learnpedia Support Personnel for technical support to resolve a question or problem report regarding the Services.
  • 5.7 "Service Unusable" is any situation where Customer is unable to access or use the Services for the majority of its End Users. Customer must identify a Request as Service Unusable by designating it as a P1 Priority support Request.
  • 5.8 "Standard Request" means a Request made by Customer to Learnpedia that is not a Service Unusable Request or Feature Request.
